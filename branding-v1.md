# AreaSorted - Branding v1

> Alignment note (2026-03-16)
> - Authoritative product direction is `AreaSorted` as a managed marketplace with `provider-company` as the primary commercial entity.
> - `ProviderCompany` is the top-level provider model for customer booking, pricing, onboarding, admin review, Stripe setup, and provider portal access.
> - `Cleaner` or worker flows remain legacy or secondary operational modules unless a document explicitly states they are future subcontractor/workforce features under a provider.
> - Provider auth lifecycle should be read as: `invite -> email verification -> password setup -> onboarding -> admin review -> Stripe -> pricing -> active portal`.
> - Where this document still references older names such as `WashHub`, `Alder London`, or `London Cleaning Platform`, treat them as legacy wording pending full content rewrite; `AreaSorted` is the active brand.


## Brand Goal

Build a brand that feels:
- trustworthy enough for customers to pay online
- credible enough for provider partners to complete onboarding and portal setup
- local enough to feel relevant in London
- polished enough to support SEO and long-term growth

## Alignment Clarification

- `AreaSorted` is the active brand
- Older brand explorations such as `Alder London` are legacy naming explorations only

## Brand Positioning

This should not feel like:
- a cheap leaflet cleaning company
- a generic startup app
- a discount marketplace

This should feel like:
- a modern London service brand
- organized, professional, verified, reliable
- human and warm, but not casual or messy

## Brand Personality

- reliable
- calm
- polished
- practical
- local
- respectful

## Tone Of Voice

- clear and direct
- warm but professional
- no exaggerated promises
- no slang-heavy copy on the main website
- recruitment pages should sound supportive and legitimate

## Brand Naming Direction

Best naming style for this business:
- short
- easy to say
- easy to spell
- sounds trustworthy in the UK market
- works for both customers and cleaners

Avoid names that feel:
- too cheap
- too gimmicky
- too tech-startup
- too narrow if you later expand beyond one borough

## Recommended Naming Routes

### Route A - Premium London Service
Best for:
- trust
- stronger long-term brand value
- works for SEO and recruitment

Examples of naming feel:
- Verra
- Alder

- Hale

### Route B - Clear Cleaning Brand
Best for:
- instant understanding
- easier early conversion

Examples of naming feel:
- London House Clean
- London Home Clean
- Prime Domestic London

### Route C - Trust + Home Feel
Best for:
- household audience
- recurring domestic cleaning

Examples of naming feel:
- Hearth & Home Cleaning
- House & Haven
- Haven Domestic

## My Recommended Route

Use `Route A - Premium London Service`.

Reason:
- easier to look credible on Indeed
- easier to look trustworthy when requesting ID and work permit documents
- easier to build a better-looking website
- easier to expand into premium domestic and office cleaning later

## Visual Brand Direction Recommendation

Recommended direction:
- warm stone background
- deep green or charcoal as primary color
- muted brass or terracotta accent
- editorial-style typography
- clean, structured layouts with strong spacing

Recommended font pairing direction:
- headline: `Fraunces` or `Cormorant Garamond`
- body: `Manrope` or `Public Sans`

## Logo Direction

Do not start with a complicated icon.

Start with:
- strong wordmark
- optional monogram
- simple emblem later if needed

Good early logo style:
- elegant serif or refined sans wordmark
- subtle spacing
- no obvious broom/house cliche icon unless very restrained

## Brand Rules

- avoid purple-heavy startup colors
- avoid bright cheap blue-and-yellow cleaner branding
- avoid cartoon cleaning icons
- avoid fake luxury black-and-gold look
